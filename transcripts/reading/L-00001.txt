April 10, 1919

Dearest Darling Blanche,

I received your letter that you wrote Tuesday yesterday morning about nine o'clock, and it was stamped at 8:30 Tuesday night in Nashville, so it sure did make a quick trip, didn't it.

We passed our exam yesterday and signed our discharges and also the payroll last night, and they say we will get our discharge and all out if nothing happens, and I sure hope we get them soon that morning so I can catch #2 or the [train?].

Please fly out so I can get to Nashville safe [this evening?], so we can fly together Saturday night and we can go out home Sunday. Just think — only two more days and then we can look each other [in the face?] and just keep on loving each other and not have to be studying about when we have to depart again.

Oh honey, I just want you to always love me, and when I am tired or feeling bad I will be perfectly happy if you will just put your arms around me and kiss me.
